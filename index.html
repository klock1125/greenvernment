Our Problem

Government policies are not harsh enough on greenhouse gas emitting activities, causing industries to not combat one of the biggest environmental problems right now. Policymakers are not increasing the price of greenhouse gas emitting activities and are not increasing funding for research to lower the cost of low-emission energy sources. Together, lenient policies and lack of funding cause low motivation to address greenhouse gas emissions, one of the greatest factors in the current climate crisis. (earth.org)

What are Greenhouse Gases?

Each year, humans produce around 50 billion tonnes of greenhouse gases, but what are greenhouse gases exactly?
Greenhouse gases are gases such as methane, nitrous oxide, and water vapor that trap heat in Earthâ€™s atmosphere, acting like the walls of a garden greenhouse. These gases are released into the environment when fossil fuels are burned. (nationalgrid.com) An unhealthy amount of greenhouse gas emissions causes global warming and climate change instead of keeping the Earth at a moderate temperature. (ourworldindata.org)

Significance

Carbon pricing measures the costs of greenhouse gas emissions that is paid for, such as healthcare costs from heat waves or droughts, damage to property from rising sea levels and floods, etc., usually in the form of a price on the carbon dioxide emitted. (worldbank.org) However, according to the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (oecd.org), fossil fuel subsidies, which release huge amounts of greenhouse gases when burned, have decreased their carbon price by 20% since 2018, to an average of of 0.12 euros per tonne of carbon dioxide.

What You Can Do

We encourage you to use your voice and take action to pressure policymakers through these methods:
Vote - Citizens over 18 should vote as their civic duty, we encourage you to vote for senators and representatives that have the same values as you and place the same importance on environmental policies.
Call, Email, Write - Find your representatives' or senators' phone numbers, emails, and addresses through their website, and state your cause for improving environmental policies.

Our Data

This bar graph showing the top ten industries adding to the emission of greenhouse gases models that several changes can still be made that will decrease emissions. Coming in at the top is solid waste landfill with 10.99% of greenhouse gas emissions. Because several of these industries are under government corporations, this indicates how lawmakers must continue creating policies to lowering the levels and preventing the spread of greenhouse gas emissions each year.


This line graph shows the emission of greenhouse gases in millions of kilotons from 1990-2020 in the US. Throughout the recent years, emissions have been slowly decreasing, but this does not mean lawmakers must stop preventing the increase in emissions. In order to maintain an equilibrium between greenhouse gas production and the number of years, more action must be taken to find solutions through governance.
